# VaultMage Commercialization Strategy

## Core Positioning

VaultMage should center on one clear promise:

`Use the cards I own first. Then tell me exactly what to buy.`

This is a stronger commercial position than "AI deckbuilder" or "collection tracker" alone.

VaultMage should be positioned as:

`The fastest way to turn your Magic collection into better decks and actionable purchase lists.`

Strong landing-page style messaging:

- Build decks from the cards you already own
- Know exactly what to buy next
- Upgrade any deck under your budget
- Turn your collection into a playable deck in minutes
- Scan your collection, improve your decks, and generate a buy list in one flow

Recommended core positioning line:

`VaultMage scans your collection, builds better decks from what you own, and generates the exact cards you need to buy.`

## Market Advantage

VaultMage's most defensible wedge is not generic AI.

The sharper advantage is:

`Collection-aware AI deck building and deck improvement with buy-list generation.`

That means:

- use owned cards first
- identify missing cards automatically
- recommend cuts and additions
- generate a prioritized purchase list
- optimize around a user-defined budget
- export or deep-link users into a purchase flow

This is stronger than a normal deck recommendation engine because it is interactive and personalized to the user's collection and deck context.

## Product Thesis

The commercial version of VaultMage should focus on this user flow:

1. Import or scan collection
2. Build or improve a deck
3. Mark owned vs missing cards
4. Optimize for a budget
5. Generate a purchase list
6. Export or deep-link to buy

The strongest product promise is:

`Build the best deck you can from your collection, then buy only the missing cards.`

## Feature Spec

### Core User Flows

#### 1. Build From My Collection

User inputs:

- format
- commander or color identity
- playstyle
- budget
- preferred store

System behavior:

- generate a deck using owned cards first
- identify missing cards needed to finish the deck
- rank purchases by priority
- show estimated total cost

#### 2. Improve This Deck

User selects an existing deck and VaultMage returns:

- cuts
- adds
- owned-card replacements
- missing upgrades to buy
- total estimated spend

#### 3. Finish This Deck

User provides or selects a target deck and VaultMage returns:

- cards already owned
- cards still missing
- cheapest completion path
- estimated completion cost

#### 4. Upgrade By Budget

User sets a budget such as:

- $25
- $50
- $100

VaultMage returns:

- best upgrades per dollar
- which cards to cut
- which cards to add
- what can be done only from owned cards
- what must be purchased

#### 5. Buy List Generation

Output should include:

- owned cards
- missing cards
- must-buy cards
- optional upgrades
- estimated total spend
- cheapest completion path
- export or store-ready output

### High-Value Features To Prioritize

- store preference: TCGplayer, Card Kingdom, Cardmarket
- card version preferences: cheapest, matching set, foil/non-foil
- upgrade tiers: essential, strong, luxury
- budget modes:
  - complete as cheaply as possible
  - maximize power under budget
  - only use my collection
- purchase exports:
  - CSV
  - copyable text list
  - store-ready cart/export where possible

## Monetization Model

Best model:

`Freemium + AI upsell + affiliate revenue`

Recommended pricing:

- Free
- Pro: $3.99/month or $29.99/year
- AI Plus: $8.99/month or $69.99/year

If VaultMage launches with only one paid tier:

- Pro: $4.99/month or $39.99/year

Recommended rationale:

- utility features alone usually price in the low monthly range
- AI usage creates real marginal cost and should be monetized separately
- affiliate revenue on purchases can subsidize free users and improve economics

### Revenue Stack

- subscription revenue for utility and AI features
- affiliate or partner revenue on outbound card purchases
- future power-user revenue from advanced analytics, finance tools, or premium exports

## Free Vs Paid

### Free Tier

Free should be good enough to spread organically.

Suggested free features:

- card search
- collection tracking
- manual deck building
- basic scan quota
- deck value view
- limited AI uses per month
- limited buy-list generation

### Pro Tier

Pro should remove utility friction.

Suggested Pro features:

- unlimited collection and deck features
- unlimited scans
- advanced filters and stats
- price history and collection analytics
- unlimited exports
- saved budget plans
- price-drop alerts
- deck-completion alerts

### AI Plus Tier

AI Plus should capture the core differentiation.

Suggested AI Plus features:

- high-cap or unlimited AI deck builds
- deck improvement chat
- owned-card-first optimization
- budget-constrained upgrade plans
- ranked buy lists
- "complete this deck from cheapest sources"
- "best upgrades for $X" workflows

Suggested initial usage gate:

- Free: 3 AI generations per month
- Pro: utility-focused, optionally 5-10 AI generations per month
- AI Plus: high monthly cap with fair-use protections

## What To Sell

VaultMage should not market "AI" as the primary message.

It should sell outcomes:

- save time
- avoid duplicate buys
- stay on budget
- turn a collection into a playable deck
- convert a recommendation into an actionable checkout list

The clearest commercial framing is:

`VaultMage helps you build better Magic decks from the cards you already own, then tells you exactly what to buy.`

## Competitive Framing

VaultMage should avoid presenting itself as "another ManaBox."

Instead, the comparison should be:

- collection apps help users organize cards
- deck sites help users discover lists
- recommendation engines help users find nearby decks
- VaultMage helps users act on their real collection and budget

In short:

`VaultMage is a collection-to-purchase decision engine.`

## MVP Priorities

To make the commercial version compelling, prioritize these in order:

1. reliable owned vs missing card resolution
2. budget-aware buy-list generation
3. export or deep-link purchase flow
4. strong deck-improvement outputs with cuts and adds
5. store and version preferences

## Suggested V1 User Experience

Ideal flow:

1. User scans or imports collection
2. User selects "Build" or "Improve"
3. User enters budget and store preference
4. VaultMage generates a deck plan
5. App labels owned, missing, and optional cards
6. User taps "Buy Missing Cards"
7. VaultMage exports the purchase list or sends the user to a supported store flow

## Summary

VaultMage's strongest commercial angle is:

`Build and improve decks from the cards I actually own, then generate the exact cards I need to buy.`

That is the right product wedge, the right monetization anchor, and the clearest distinction from broader collection/deckbuilding tools.
